Mushi3D Printer

The Mushi3D printer is designed and build by me. Originally designed to fit exactly underneath my dorm room bed, this FDM 3D printer was my passion project while I completed my undergraduate studies. Consisting of a project-steel frame, tempered glass bed and many 3D printed parts, it has an available print area of 1.3 x 1.2 x 0.8 meters and runs Marlin 2.1.1 firmware.

The print bed of the Mushi3D printer was its largest engineering challenge. Going through several design iterations and 100+ 3D printed parts, the process of creating a functional Z-axis print bed of this unusual scale took over a year. The current implementation weighs just over 100 pounds and is lifted by 4 stepper motors with the bulk of its weight offset by 4 constant force springs. Prints adhere excellently to the tempered-glass bed which is manually levelled at 16 points.

Some upcoming projects on this huge printer are: 3D printing small vehicle chassis such as a go-cart or bike frame and printing life-size prints of some 3D scans of my colleagues.

ChildrensHeart VR Medical Scan Viewer

The ChildrensHeart Virtual Reality application was built at the reqeust of the University of Arkansas for Medical Sciences Children's Hospital. It allows for the immersive examination of medical imaging data from MRI's and other machines which export to STL and DICOM format. The application displays files as variable-density volumetric models or shells and supports slicing them to see internal features. It was made for the HTC Vive headset and can be explored in first person.

360 Tours

I have worked on and managed teams in the creation of several 360 degree immersive tours. These tours were created as a response to the COVID-19 Pandemic and serve as a way for prospective students and others to safely explore the UA Little Rock campus from the safety of their home or in Virtual Reality. I both programmed the user interface for the tours and captured most of the images using an Insta 360 Pro camera. These tours can be explored through web browsers, the Oculus/Meta Quest 2, and soon Google Cardboard. All of these tours can be found here: https://eac2-ualr.org/EAC-Project360/
